# Research Notes

The project was shaped by a short 2026 trend scan before implementation.

## Signals Used

1. The 2026 Stanford AI Index highlights ongoing AI adoption and public/expert gaps around work, economy, medicine, and regulation.  
   Source: https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report

2. Lightcast's Stanford AI Index analysis reports that agentic AI skill mentions grew from 0.06% of postings in 2024 to 0.23% in 2025, more than 280% growth.  
   Source: https://lightcast.io/resources/blog/stanford-ai-2026

3. MLflow's 2026 production-agent guidance emphasizes that reliable agents need architecture, governance, evaluation, and observability, not only good prompts.  
   Source: https://mlflow.org/articles/building-production-ready-ai-agents-in-2026/

4. A 2026 digital-twin survey frames AI-enabled digital twins as moving through modeling, mirroring, intervention, and autonomous management.  
   Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01321

## Design Implications

- The project should look beyond a chatbot and show agentic decision-making.
- The planner should expose rationale, cost, confidence, and forecast effects.
- The product should combine digital-twin visualization with intervention, not just monitoring.
- The app should run without external credentials so it can be judged anywhere.

## Why Crisis Response

Crisis response makes tradeoffs visible: speed versus cost, population equity versus critical infrastructure, and short-term mitigation versus forecasted stability. That gives reviewers something deeper to inspect than a static dashboard.
